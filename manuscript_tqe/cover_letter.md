# Cover letter — IEEE Transactions on Quantum Engineering

Dear Editors,

We submit for your consideration our manuscript "Kernel Geometry Governs
Robustness under Distribution Shift: A Controlled Multi-Dataset Study of
Quantum and Classical Kernels".

The paper reports a controlled kernel-swap benchmark of fidelity-based quantum
kernels against classical kernels under distribution shift, together with a
mechanistic account of the observed behavior. Within each experimental setting
the classifier, preprocessing, split construction, model-selection logic, and
bandwidth-tuning freedom are held fixed while only the kernel varies. The
protocol is instantiated on four public security benchmarks spanning two data
modalities and three drift mechanisms (including natural regime drift on
network traffic), two classifier families (SVC and a Laplace-approximation
Gaussian process classifier providing calibrated uncertainty), and 72 principal
settings with 15 repeated runs each, evaluated with paired Wilcoxon tests under
Holm correction.

Three aspects may be of particular interest to TQE readers. First, the study
was designed to resolve the criticisms most commonly raised against
quantum-kernel benchmarks: it includes probabilistic classifiers and a broad
classical reference family beyond linear/RBF, spans multiple datasets and drift
mechanisms, and accompanies the empirical comparison with a mechanism ---
survival of kernel-target alignment under shift, enabled by high effective rank
of the training Gram matrix --- that predicts within-setting out-of-distribution
accuracy across every dataset studied. Second, the bandwidth analysis
operationalizes the findings of Shaydulin and Wild (PRA 2022) and Canatar et
al. (TMLR 2023) as a protocol-fairness requirement, and shows that asymmetric
tuning freedom systematically biases quantum-versus-classical comparisons ---
against the quantum family. Third, the study is fully reproducible: every
number and figure in the paper is generated by scripts in a public archived
artifact (GitHub + Zenodo DOI), from split construction to statistical tables.

We believe the manuscript is a natural fit for TQE's coverage of quantum
computation, information, and software engineering, and complements recent TQE
publications in this genre, notably Alvarez-Estevez, "Benchmarking Quantum
Machine Learning Kernel Training for Classification Tasks" (TQE 2025), and
Park and Simeone, "Quantum Conformal Prediction for Reliable Uncertainty
Quantification in Quantum Machine Learning" (TQE 2024).

All quantum kernels are computed by exact statevector simulation; the paper
makes no hardware claim, and its scope statement addresses this explicitly.
The manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. A preprint will be posted
on arXiv in accordance with IEEE policy.

Thank you for your consideration.

On behalf of all authors,
